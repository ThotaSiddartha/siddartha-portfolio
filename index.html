Leveraging EfficientNetB4 Model with Multi-head Attentions for Maize Leaf Disease Detection

Publication Date:

September 2025

Description:

Developed an AI-powered automated system for early detection of maize leaf diseases using advanced deep learning with multi-head attention mechanisms. This research addresses critical challenges in corn cultivation that significantly impact global food security, as maize serves as a staple crop for millions worldwide.

Key Contributions:

  • Novel integration of EfficientNetB4 with multi-head attention achieving 96.75% F1-score and 96.63% accuracy in disease classification.
  • Comprehensive comparative analysis of 5 state-of-the-art CNN architectures (EfficientNetB4, DenseNet201, InceptionV3, MobileNetV3, XceptionNet).
  • Trained and validated models on 4,000 maize leaf images across 4 disease categories: Bercak Daun, Daun Sehat, Hawar Sehat, and Karat Sehat.
  • Achieved 3%+ improvement over existing state-of-the-art methods across all evaluation metrics.
  • Optimized multi-head attention with 8 attention heads and key_dim=64 for maximum feature extraction.

Technical Approach:

Employed transfer learning with compound scaling, strategic dense layers and data augmentation techniques. The fine-tuned model with dual attention layers outperformed all competing architectures.

Impact:

Enables farmers to identify crop diseases (grey leaf spot, maize rust, northern blight) early, preventing significant agricultural losses and supporting food security through precision agriculture technologies.

Conference Details:

Conference: International Conference Proceedings

Type: Peer-reviewed Conference Paper

Domain: 锔 Computer Vision, Deep Learning, Smart Agriculture

Paddy Leaf Disease Detection Using Fine-tuned EfficientNetB4 Convolutional Neural Network

Publication Date:

June 2024

Description:

Developed an AI-powered automated system for early detection of paddy leaf diseases using advanced deep learning techniques. This research addresses critical challenges in rice cultivation that affect global food security, as rice serves as a staple food for over half the world's population.

Key Contributions:

  • Implemented and fine-tuned EfficientNetB4 Convolutional Neural Network achieving 99.75% accuracy in disease classification.
  • Conducted comprehensive comparative analysis of 5 state-of-the-art CNN architectures (EfficientNetB4, MobileNetV3, DenseNet201, InceptionNetV3, XceptionNet).
  • Trained and validated models on dataset of 5,932 paddy leaf images across 4 major disease categories: Bacterial Blight, Blast, Brown Spot, and Tungro.
  • Achieved exceptional F1-score of 99.74% with perfect classification for Blast and Brown Spot diseases.
  • Demonstrated significant improvement over traditional manual detection methods.

Technical Approach:

Employed transfer learning with compound scaling methodology, custom dense layers, and data augmentation techniques. The fine-tuned EfficientNetB4 model outperformed all competing architectures with lowest test loss (0.0097) and highest accuracy metrics across precision, recall, and F1-score.

Impact:

This automated disease detection system enables farmers to identify crop diseases early, potentially reducing significant agricultural losses and supporting global food security initiatives through precision agriculture technologies.

Conference Details:

Conference: IEEE ICCCNT 2024, IIT Mandi, India

Publication Type: Peer-reviewed Conference Paper

Research Domain: 锔 Computer Vision, Deep Learning, Precision Agriculture
